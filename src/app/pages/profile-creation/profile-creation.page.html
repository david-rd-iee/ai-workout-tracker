ATLAS 8 WEEK GUARANTEE PROGRAM The Atlas Guarantee Program (the “Program”) is a remedy offered by Atlas to eligible clients (“Participants”) engaged in in-person training whose diligent adherence to an approved plan fails to produce objectively measurable results. Participants must notify their assigned Atlas Professional of their intent to enroll in the Program no later than their first live training session. Failure to notify the Atlas Professional by that time renders the Participant ineligible for the Program. At enrollment, the Participant and Atlas Professional must complete baseline assessments: (i) a body-weight measurement recorded on an NTEP-certified scale within the Atlas Platform, and (ii) logged documentation on the Atlas Platform of initial strength or performance benchmarks (e.g., resistance, repetitions, distances, durations, or intensity), which serve as the reference points for evaluating progress. The Program’s initial period is eight weeks (the “Initial Term”). During the Initial Term, the Participant must attend at least two in-person training sessions per week and comply with not less than ninety percent (90%) of all prescribed directives. Compliance is documented through workout logs, Atlas Professional notes, and exercise acknowledgments within the Atlas Platform (“Adherence Records”). “Measurable progress” is defined as either (a) a net change in body weight of at least one (1) pound from the baseline measurement, or (b) an improvement of at least one (1) whole unit in a strength or performance benchmark compared to the baseline measurement—for example, lifting one (1) additional pound, completing one (1) additional repetition, running one (1) second faster, or covering one (1) additional foot in distance, all as compared to the baseline measurements recorded at enrollment. If any of the above criteria is achieved, the Program obligations are considered met and the guarantee remedy will not apply. If a Participant is injured during the Initial Term, the Program is suspended and may resume once written medical clearance for unrestricted activity is provided. The Participant must then restart the full eight-week Initial Term with all requirements applying anew. The restart must occur with the same Atlas Professional; if that Atlas Professional is unavailable, Atlas will assign another. In such case, session costs remain the same or may be adjusted to the new Atlas Professional’s rates. If no mutually agreeable Atlas Professional or rate is available, the Participant may decline and exit the Program without penalty. Only one suspension and restart is permitted, except that Atlas may, at its discretion, allow additional restarts under extraordinary circumstances. If the Participant becomes sick, travels, or is otherwise unable to maintain ninety percent (90%) adherence, the Program must be restarted from the beginning.
